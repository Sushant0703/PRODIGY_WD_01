Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is a symbol of eternal love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, or the "Palace of Winds," is a stunning red and pink sandstone palace in Jaipur, India. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, it features unique latticed windows designed to allow royal ladies to observe street festivities while remaining hidden from public view.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism located in Amritsar, India. Its golden dome, surrounded by a sacred pool of water, reflects both spiritual significance and architectural brilliance.
